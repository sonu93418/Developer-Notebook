About the Author

Oct. 24, 2024

It started, as most things do, with a mild curiosity and a significant lack of supervision. I have spent the better part of a decade trying to make silicon rocks think, or at least pretend to think in a way that feels helpful.

I view code less as an engineering discipline and more as a strange form of modern poetry. It is structural, it has rules, but there is a definite art to making it elegant.

When I am not staring into the abyss of an IDE, I am usually found trying to master the perfect pour-over coffee and rereading dusty sci-fi novels.
